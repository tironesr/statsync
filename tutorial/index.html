Step 1: The Live Bridge

Connect R directly to Word for instant updates.

Launch the Bridge

Start a local session in R. This creates a real-time canal for your data to flow into Word.

# In R Console
library(statsync)
sync_serve(project_name = "My Thesis")

Push & Manage

Passing raw models to sync_update() automatically extracts and refreshes data in Word. You can also prune or clear your session.

# Refresh specific models
sync_update(model1, model2)

# Prune stale models (no quotes needed)
sync_delete(old_model)

# Clear all models from the bridge
sync_clear()

# Stop the bridge
sync_stop()

Pro Tip: sync_update() automatically handles statistical extraction. You only need to pass the models you want to refresh in the live feed.

The Magic of Auto-Update

Linked coefficients update themselves.

Zero-Effort Refresh

Once you've inserted a statistic into Word, it stays linked. If you change your data or refine your model in R, just call sync_update() again.

Every instance of that model's coefficients in your Word document will update automatically to the latest values.

# Update model - Word reflects this instantly!
sync_update(model1)
